Former Super Eagles goalkeeper Ike Shorunmu names who Gernot Rohr should not invite for Benin, Lesot

- Ike Shorunmu has urged Super Eagles co'ach Rohr not to invite Ahmed Musa for Benin, Lesotho games

- The Nigerian team captain Musa has been out of club since leaving Al Nassr in October

- Shorunmu warned that he does not see the need to invite someone who has been out of club for months

Former Super Eagles goalkeeper Ike Shorunmu has expressed concerns over the attempt by the Super Eagles coach Gernot Rohr to invite captain Ahmed Musa to the squad.

Musa has been out of action for a period of five months ahead of the forthcoming Africa Cup of Nations qualifiers against Benin and Lesotho.

Ahmed Musa was a part of the squad that played against Sierra Leone in the doubleheader fixtures.

The Nigerian international left Saudi outfit Al Nassr in October 2020 and is still in search of a club, despite interest from some Premier League teams.

With Gernot Rohr expected to release names of players for the matches any moment, Shorunmu has clarified that the decision to or not invite Musa is all in the hands of the manager as he knows what will be best for the game.

He went further to state that due to the player’s long absence from games spanning over 5 months, Musa could become a liability rather than an asset.

Shorunmu told Punch via Complete Sports:

“It is left for the coach to invite whoever he feels he needs into the team. I however don’t think it is the best thing to invite someone who has not been playing for about five months to the national team. "You don’t expect such a player to be in top form and shape.”
