Zamfara governor reacts to allegation of appointing Hadeija Bashir

The Zamfara State government has found itself at the centre of a controversy involving Bashir Hadejia, a high-profile suspect recently arrested on charges of gunrunning and smuggling large caches of weapons.

Allegations have emerged suggesting that Hadejia was an appointee of the Zamfara State Government, a claim the government has vehemently denied.

A letter, dated October 27, 2023, purportedly signed by Mal Abubakar M. Nakwada, the Secretary to the Zamfara State Government, stated that Hadejia had been appointed as the Technical Adviser, Mining, under the Office of the Executive Governor.

The letter read, "I am pleased to inform you that His Excellency the Governor of Zamfara State, Dauda Lawal, has approved your appointment at Government House...based on recognition of your experience, competence, and dedication to duty."

The letter further indicated that Hadejia's appointment was effective from October 27, 2023, and that he would receive a salary according to the terms of service guiding his role.

The document also expressed the hope that Hadejia would justify the confidence placed in him by the governor.

Zamfara govt denies Hadejia's appointment

However, a source in the State House in Zamfara authoritatively told Pulse Nigeria that the document was forged and disowned its authenticity.

"This document does not exist in the Zamfara State government's records. It is a mischievous attempt by some political rivals to link my principal with the Hadejia saga," the source stated in a WhatsApp message to Pulse Nigeria.
